Abstract

The evolving capabilities of large language models (LLMs) have outpaced traditional evaluation methodologies and introduced novel evaluation challenges, such as assessing human-like psychological constructs, addressing the limitations of static and task-specific benchmarks, and meeting the requirement for human-centered evaluation. These challenges intersect with psychometrics, the science of quantifying intangible aspects of human psychology. This survey introduces and synthesizes the emerging interdisciplinary field of LLM psychometrics, which applies psychometric instruments, theories, and principles to evaluate, understand, and enhance LLMs. We systematically review the role of psychometrics in shaping LLM benchmarking principles, broadening evaluation scopes, refining evaluation methodologies, validating assessment processes, and enhancing LLM capabilities. The integration of diverse efforts and insights in this paper offers a structured framework for researchers from various backgrounds, facilitating a comprehensive understanding of this emerging field. We aim to offer insights into developing future evaluation paradigms for human-level AI and advance human-centered AI psychology for the greater common good.

Measuring Psychological Constructs

Figure 2: Examples of psychometric tests for LLMs, showing both personality assessments (left) and cognitive evaluations (right).

LLMs exhibit measurable personality traits and cognitive capabilities that emerge from their training. Our survey comprehensively maps these constructs, showing how different assessment frameworks can be applied to evaluate various aspects of LLM psychology, from personality traits like the Big Five to complex cognitive abilities like Theory of Mind.

Evaluation Methodology

Figure 3: Overview of LLM psychometric evaluation methodology, including test formats, data sources, prompting strategies, and scoring mechanisms.

Our survey systematically examines the methodological approaches for psychometric evaluation of LLMs. This includes structured tests, open-ended conversations, and agentic simulations, alongside diverse data sources ranging from established inventories to synthetic items generated by LLMs themselves. We analyze various prompting strategies and scoring mechanisms that enhance the reliability and validity of the assessment process.

Psychometric Validation

Figure 4: Overview of psychometric validation: reliability and consistency, validity, and standards and recommendations.

Ensuring the reliability and validity of LLM assessments presents unique challenges. Our survey explores test-retest reliability, content validity, construct validity, and criterion validity in the context of LLM evaluation. We present empirical findings showing how prompt sensitivity, social desirability bias, and other factors influence assessment results.

LLM Enhancement Techniques

Psychometric principles extend beyond evaluating LLMs; they serve as powerful tools for model development and enhancement. Our survey explores how psychometric frameworks can shape and enhance LLM behaviors across three key domains:

  • Trait Manipulation: Techniques for controlling LLM personality expressions through prompting, inference-time interventions, and fine-tuning
  • Safety and Alignment: Leveraging psychometric measurements to guide alignment with human values and improve safety
  • Cognitive Enhancement: Methods for developing more human-like reasoning, empathy, and communication capabilities

These applications demonstrate how a deep understanding of LLM psychology can inform the development of more capable, aligned, and beneficial AI systems.

Future Directions

Our survey identifies several emerging trends and future directions for LLM psychometrics research:

  • From Human Constructs to LLM Constructs: Developing new psychological theories specifically tailored to LLMs
  • Perceived vs. Aligned Traits: Distinguishing between traits perceived in text and those aligned with human values
  • Anthropomorphization Challenges: Addressing methodological issues in treating LLMs as entities with psychological traits
  • Expanding Dimensions: Extending psychometric evaluation to multilingual, multi-turn, multimodal, and multi-agent contexts
  • Item Response Theory: Leveraging advanced psychometric models to improve evaluation efficiency and accuracy
